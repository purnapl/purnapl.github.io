Pelmeni

From momsdish.com pelmeni recipe

Dough
3 cups flour
2/3 cup lukewarm water
3 eggs
1 teaspoon salt

Filling
2 pounds ground beef
1 large onion, grated
1-2 teaspoons salt
Pepper

Topping
¼ cup siracha
¼ cup white vinegar
Butter
Sour cream
Curry powder

Make the dough: combine all ingredients in a bowl and knead into a smooth dough. Cover with a damp towel or plastic bag and set aside at room temperature for at least 30 minutes.

Make the filling: combine all ingredients in a large bowl and mix well. Refrigerate while you let the dough rest.

When the dough is done resting, roll it out dough very thin (less than 1/8 of an inch). Cut out circles about 2-3 inches in diameter using a jar. Put a spoonful of filling in the middle, wet the edges, and fold over to create a half-circle. Connect the ends of the half moon to shape the pelmeni.

Cook the pelmeni in lightly salted simmering water; the pelmeni are cooked when they float to the surface (but you can cook them an extra 30 seconds to 1 minute after they float to the surface to be sure they are cooked).

Mix siracha and white vinegar together. Top pelmeni with a little bit of butter, curry powder, siracha-vinegar sauce, and sour cream.

Eggplant Parmesan

2-3 large eggplants
Salt
2 eggs, whisked with a few tablespoons of water
Flour
Breadcrumbs or panko
Garlic powder
Oregano, thyme, rosemary, sage, or a combination of all three, finely chopped or crushed
Cayenne
Parmesan
Vegetable oil
1 container ricotta
Mozzarella, grated or finely chopped

Slice eggplants thinly (1/8-1/4 inch thick) crosswise. Place eggplant circles in a single layer on cutting board or baking tray and sprinkle salt over them. Let sit at least 15 minutes, flip, and salt the other side. Let sit another 15 minutes, then rinse thoroughly. Dry off with a towel.

Heat about ½ inch vegetable oil in a frying pan. Mix breadcrumbs, crushed herbs, garlic powder, pepper, and parmesan. Dip each eggplant slice in flour. Shake off, then dip in egg and again in breadcrumb mixture. Fry until lightly brown on each side. Set aside.

Once all eggplant slices are fried, place them in single layer on the bottom of an oiled pan. Mix ricotta, crushed spices, garlic powder, pepper, parmesan, and salt. Spread ricotta mixture on top of eggplant slices in pan, add another layer of eggplant slices on top, and add a layer of tomato sauce on top of the eggplant. Repeat the layers: eggplant, ricotta, eggplant, tomato sauce, until you are out of eggplant slices. On top of the final layer of eggplant, add tomato sauce, mozzarella, parmesan, and herbs. Bake at 425F until the top is crispy, and serve alongside pasta.

Pesto

1/3 -½ cup cashews (lightly toasted)
2 cloves garlic
4 tablespoons olive oil
3 cups basil leaves (loosely packed)
Juice of ¼-½ lemon
1-2 tablespoons water (if using blender)
½ teaspoon sugar (optional, use if cashews are less sweet)
Salt to taste

Combine cashews, garlic, olive oil, basil, and juice of about ¼ lemon in a blender or food processor. If using a blender, also add 1 tablespoon water to make it easier to blend; add more if necessary to facilitate blending. Blend until mixture reaches desired consistency, then taste and stir in salt, sugar, and lemon juice as desired.

Pesto pasta salad with tomatoes, mozzarella, and chicken

About 4-6 cups cooked short pasta (spirals, bowties, etc.)
1-2 medium tomatoes (chopped), or ½-1 pint cherry tomatoes (halved)
At least 5-6 small balls fresh mozzarella, quartered
About ½ cup fresh pesto or slightly less jarred pesto (save empty pesto blender or jar for next step)
Parmesan (optional)
Salt (to taste)

Pesto vinaigrette chicken

2-3 boneless chicken thighs, chopped into small (½ -1 inch) pieces
3 cloves garlic, chopped
Splash of olive oil
Salt, to taste
Pepper, to taste
Everyday seasoning, to taste
Garlic powder, to taste
1-2 tablespoons lemon juice
1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar

Cook the pasta. While it is cooking, chop the tomatoes, mozzarella, and chicken. Drain pasta and run cold water over it to cool it down, then mix with tomatoes, mozzarella, and pesto.

Heat a frying pan with a splash of oil or olive oil; when the oil is hot, add the chicken, garlic, and generous amounts of salt, pepper, everyday seasoning, and garlic powder. While the chicken is cooking, place lemon juice and balsamic in unwashed pesto blender or empty pesto jar; if you don’t have an empty unwashed pesto blender or jar, mix in about a tablespoon of pesto with the lemon juice and balsamic. Cap container and shake to blend pesto with lemon juice and vinegar (and clean container in the process!). When chicken is cooked, pour pesto vinaigrette mixture over chicken and turn off heat; the sauce will quickly reduce to coat the chicken.

Cool chicken briefly and combine with rest of pasta salad. Add salt and/or parmesan to taste.

Roast chicken with potatoes and mushrooms

Chicken

Olive oil
Lemon juice
3-4 garlic cloves, crushed and chopped
Salt
Freshly ground black pepper
Everyday seasoning
Smoked paprika and/or cayenne
Thyme leaves or finely chopped rosemary (optional)
2-3 chicken thighs (preferable bone-in)

Potatoes

Enough potatoes to fill pan, chopped into approximately ½ inch places
Olive oil
Salt
Garlic powder
Everyday seasoning
Freshly ground black pepper
Everyday seasoning
Smoked paprika and/or cayenne
Finely chopped rosemary
Thyme leaves (optional)

Mushrooms

Enough brown mushrooms to fill small cast iron pan, quartered
2-3 garlic cloves, crushed and chopped
1-2 tablespoons butter, divided into 6-8 small pieces
Salt
Everyday seasoning
Freshly ground black pepper

Mix all the chicken ingredients except for the chicken together in medium-sized bowl, taste, adjust, and add chicken, turning to coat in marinade. Cook immediately or refrigerate until ready to cook.

Preheat oven to 425F and combine all potato ingredients in cast iron pan. Roast until potatoes are almost tender, stir, then place the marinated chicken thighs on top (cook potatoes longer before adding chicken if using boneless thighs). Continue roasting until chicken is cooked and the skin is crispy, turning down heat if necessary to keep skin from burning.

While roasting potatoes, roast mushrooms: place all mushroom ingredients except butter in small cast iron pan. Scatter small pieces of butter on top of mushrooms, place lid on pan, and roast for about 20 minutes, then remove lid and roast until little liquid remains. Remove and serve with chicken, potatoes, and parsley sauce.

Chicken Tikka Masala

About 1 tablespoon oil
1 teaspoon garam masala
1 teaspoon cumin
1 teaspoon curry powder (and/or ½ teaspoon each of coriander and turmeric)
Dash of chili powder
1 yellow onion, chopped (more onion = sweeter curry)
2-3 cloves garlic, chopped
½-1 inch ginger, grated or finely chopped
1 teaspoon salt
1 can crushed or pureed tomatoes
Pinch of cinnamon
Pinch of cardamom
3-4 chicken thighs
1-2 tablespoons butter or heavy cream

Heat the oil in a pot or large frying pan on medium-high heat until hot, then add the spices and briefly bloom in the hot oil (less than 30 seconds). Quickly stir in the onions, ginger, and garlic. Sauté for a few minutes on medium-high or high heat, until onions are fragrant and beginning to brown in places, then stir in the salt, put a lid on the pan, and turn the heat down to medium or medium low. Cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring every few minutes, until onions are translucent and soft. Remove from heat.

Place the tomatoes/ tomato puree in a blender with the sautéed onions and puree until smooth. Pour tomato-onion mixture back into the pan and cook over medium or medium-high heat for 10-15 more minutes, until tomato tastes cooked; it should be at a simmer the entire time. Taste and add additional spices as desired during this cooking period.

Place the chicken thighs in the simmering curry mixture. Cook until chicken is fully cooked, about another 15-20 minutes, adding water as necessary to maintain the desired curry sauce consistency. When chicken is cooked, you can remove it from the pan to chop it up, or leave it in larger pieces. Turn off heat, add a splash of cream or pat of butter, and serve over rice.

Bao Buns

From BBC News Bao Buns recipe

4 ¼ cups flour
1-2 tablespoons sugar
1 teaspoon yeast
½ teaspoon salt
¼ cup milk or soymilk
1 ¼ cup water
1 tablespoon oil
1 tablespoon white vinegar
1 teaspoon baking powder

In a medium bowl, mix the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt together. Stir in water, milk, oil, and vinegar, and knead mixture into a dough, adding more water as necessary.

Turn the dough out on a lightly floured surface and knead at least 10 minutes, until smooth. Rise in an oiled bowl for 2-3 hours (depending on temperature), until doubled in size.

Punch down the dough and sprinkle the baking powder over it, then knead for a few minutes to combine. If you forget this step, the bao will still work! They might just be slightly less fluffy.

Divide dough into about 18 equally sized pieces, then roll each piece into a ball. Let the balls rest about 10 minutes, until gluten is relaxed and they are squishy and not firm.

Flatten each ball out into an oval about a finger-width thick, and fold the oval over on itself. This is how we’ll pry open the bao when it’s done. Place each folded bao onto a square of parchment or foil.

Let rise another 1-2 hours. Place a colander inside a large pot, and add 2-3 inches of water to the pot. When the water boils, add 3-5 bao on their parchment or foil squares, making sure that they are not too close together. Steam 8 minutes, then remove and steam the next batch. Pry the buns open at the seam, add desired fillings (recommended: hoisin-soy sauce chicken and ginger-cabbage salad) and serve hot.

Bao fillings

Roast hoisin-soy sauce chicken or pork

¼ cup hoisin
2 tablespoons ketchup (optional)
1/3-1/2 cup soy sauce
2-3 tablespoons brown sugar
2-4 tablespoons white vinegar
Chili paste (optional)
2-3 cloves garlic, grated
½ inch ginger, grated
4-6 chicken thighs, or an approximately equal quantity of pork riblets

Combine all ingredients except for the meat, taste, and adjust ingredient quantities to taste. Add the meat and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.

Preheat the oven to 375F. Roast chicken or pork until done; if desired, you can broil for a few minutes to blacken the top slightly. Let cool slightly, then chop into pieces and serve inside bao buns.

Eggplant-Rice Noodle Stir Fry

Tofu

1 block firm tofu
At least 1 cup cornstarch
Chili-lime powder
Garlic powder
Salt
Vegetable oil

Stir fry

1 medium-large eggplant, sliced into ½ inch chunks
Salt
6-8 brown mushrooms, sliced
Rice noodles, preferably the wide ones
Ginger, grated
3-4 garlic cloves, chopped
Soy sauce
Brown sugar
White vinegar
Hot pepper sauce (sambal)
Black pepper

Salt the eggplant: place chopped eggplant in a large bowl, salt with a good quantity of salt, and let sit while you prepare the rest of the ingredients.

Set a pot of water to boil on the stove. When it boils, cook the rice noodles, drain, and set aside.

Fry the tofu: mix all the ingredients except tofu together in a small bowl. Slice tofu into chunks of the desired size, coat in cornstarch mixture, and fry in preheated vegetable oil; do not allow the tofu to touch in the pan or they will stick together. Flip when browned and crispy on the first side, allow to brown on the second side, and remove from the pan when fully crispy. Set aside.

Rinse the eggplant to remove as much of the salt as possible. Remove all but 1-2 tablespoons of oil from the tofu-frying pan. Once it’s hot, add the eggplant and cook until slightly browned on both sides and soft. Remove the eggplant, set aside, and add mushrooms. Once the mushrooms are cooked, add ginger, and garlic. Once the garlic starts to brown, add the noodles, fried tofu, cooked eggplant, and the rest of the ingredients. Stir everything together, taste, and adjust flavorings. Serve.

Piecrust

1 ½ cup flour (1 ¼ cup flour for flakier crust)
2 tablespoons sugar
10 tablespoons butter, cold
Ice water

Mix flour and sugar together. Combine with butter using either a grater (grate frozen butter into flour); a fork and knife (chopping into small pieces); or a blender (combine in blender). Add ice water as necessary to form a ball of dough, kneading as little as possible. Place dough ball in refrigerator in a plastic bag for at least an hour.

Roll dough out on lightly floured plastic-bag covered cutting board and use to line a piepan. There should be enough left for a thin top crust or strips to weave a lattice top crust.

Claire’s 23rd Birthday Chocolate Cake

Modified from the Silver Palette’s chocolate cake recipe

1 stick plus 3 tablespoons butter, plus extra for greasing pan
1 3/4 cups plus 2 tablespoons unbleached all-purpose flour, plus extra for flouring the pan
½ cup plus 1 tablespoon cocoa powder
1 cup very hot coffee or a mixture of very hot coffee and boiling water (adjust ratio for more or less coffee flavor in cake)
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
2 cups sugar
2 eggs, separated
1 teaspoon baking soda
1/2 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt
1 teaspoon baking powder

Preheat oven to 350F. Butter a cakepan and line with parchment paper for easy cake removal.

Cut the butter into small pieces and place in a large bowl with cocoa powder. Pour hot coffee or hot coffee/boiling water mixture over butter and cocoa powder; let stand until butter is melted. Stir in the vanilla and sugar, then whisk in the egg yolks.

In a small bowl, mix the baking soda and sour cream and whisk into the chocolate mixture. Sift the flour and baking powder and add to the batter, mixing thoroughly.

Beat the egg whites until stiff but not too dry. Stir a quarter of the egg whites thoroughly into the batter. Gently fold in the remaining whites.

Pour the batter into the prepared pan. Set on the center rack of the oven and bake until a cake tester inserted into the center comes out clean. Cool in the pan for 10 minutes, unmold and cool completely before frosting.
